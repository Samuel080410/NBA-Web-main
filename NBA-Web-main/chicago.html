Team History

The Chicago Bulls are a professional basketball team based in Chicago, Illinois, founded in 1966. The team rose to global prominence in the 1990s, largely due to the leadership of Michael Jordan. Under head coach Phil Jackson, the Bulls dominated the NBA, winning six championships (1991-1993, 1996-1998). Known for their distinctive red, black, and white uniforms, the Bulls became a cultural phenomenon, with Jordan becoming one of the greatest athletes of all time. After Jordan's retirement in 1999, the Bulls experienced a period of rebuilding but remain one of the NBA's most recognized and historic franchises.
